    Every Fall, we hear the same story. Computer Science students at Northeastern University enroll in CS 1210, and get bombarded with an impossible number of tasks. Write a resume. Do side projects. Apply to 10 jobs a week. It's stressful, and you are not alone in feeling overwhelmed. We were there too.
    It's not fair for anyone to expect a sophomore in college to have a fully formed resume. Few of us have even had internships. There are a couple things you can do though, to make yourself stand apart from your peers. First and foremost is to show off the hard work you've been doing the last year and a half!
    Our founder is a recruiter for top companies in the finance and tech space, and one of the first things he looks for on any resume is a portfolio website! There's just no better way to display your work. We understand, though, that it's beyond difficult to find the time to build a portfolio site between classes and an active coop search. That's where we come in.
    Ladera Development will provide you a fully functioning portfolio website that you can link on your resume! Simply pick a template, then send us a headshot, personality picture, your LinkedIn, and links to any personal projects you may have. In 10 days or less, we'll arrange a final call, where we'll hand over the keys to your brand new site, custom domain name and all!
    At the end of the day, Ladera Development is not going to get you your coop. You still need to focus hard in your classes, and kick ass in your interviews. But setting up a portfolio site just might get your foot in the door. Are you willing to pass that up?
